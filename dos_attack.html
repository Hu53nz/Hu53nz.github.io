What is a DoS Attack on Chatbots?

In chatbots, a Denial of Service (DoS) attack aims to overload the system, causing delays or crashes that make it unavailable for regular users. Attackers might send a high volume of requests to exploit resources or bypass limitations, potentially rendering the chatbot ineffective or even damaging its functionality.

DoS Attack Examples Targeting Chatbots

  • HTTP Flooding: Bombarding the chatbot server with a large number of HTTP requests.
  • UDP Amplification: Exploiting other open servers to amplify and flood the chatbot’s network.
  • Slowloris Attack: Keeping multiple connections open to exhaust the chatbot’s available resources.
